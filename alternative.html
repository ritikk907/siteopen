Aerobics

What is Aerobics?

Aerobic which interprets to "with oxygen" is exercise to provide cardiovascular conditioning. Controling the amount of oxygen that you breath in can help you to burn those extra pounds effectively.

Why Aerobics?

While doing aerobics human body responds in an effective manner. You will breath faster and more deeply which will maximize the amount of oxygen in blood. More oxygen delivered to your muscles and increased blood flow to muscles and back to your lungs. Body will release endorphines(work as natural painkiller).

How to get started?

One should always start from taking small steps, now aerobic activity can be anything you can start to go for a 10minutes walk to start from the very begining or start with a dance or something it is imported to get started. Once, you get a hang of some sort of activity on regular basis you can increase the bar and reach the goal of 30minutes of aerobics for 5days in a week.

Zumba

What is Zumba?

According to wikipedia- Zumba is a fitness program that involves cardio and Latin-inspired dance. It was founded by Colombian dancer and choreographer Beto Pérez in 2001, and by 2012, it had 110,000 locations and 12 million people taking classes weekly. Zumba is a trademark owned by Zumba Fitness, LLC.

Why Zumba?

Grooving to the beats of salsa, flamenco, and merengue music feels more like a dance party than a workout, which is exactly what makes Zumba so popular. The Latin-inspired dance workout is one of the most popular group exercise classes in the world.There are several different kinds of Zumba classes, from Aqua Zumba workouts to classes like Zumba Toning that incorporate weights for additional calorie burning and strength training.

Pilates

What is Pilates?

According to wikipedia- mind-body exercise developed in the early 20th century by German physical trainer Joseph Pilates, after whom it was named. Pilates called his method "Contrology".[3] It is practiced worldwide, especially in countries such as Australia, Canada, South Korea, the United States and the United Kingdom. As of 2005, there were 11 million people practicing the discipline regularly and 14,000 instructors in the United States.

Why Pilates?

it helps build strength and flexibility, Wonesh says Pilates is a great way to add some functional variety into your workout routine. "Whether you're trying to get in shape or just improve your overall health, there's really no one type of exercise that can get you there," explains Wonesh. Interestingly, Pilates wasn't initially created with the idea of everyday exercise in mind. It was actually invented as way to help aid dancers through injury."The goal was to take dancers who tended to have overuse injuries from training the same motor patterns over and over again and cross train these dancers by focusing on strengthening their core muscles.

How to get started?

To get started with pilates those new to Pilates can begin with a home practice. Mat Pilates videos and books are widely available. A home practice is safe for anyone free of injury or chronic back pain. Using the videos and links below start your Journey
